## Chapter 01 - Silhouette Language

Status: Review Draft

Purpose:

Define the visual identity of every playable profession by silhouette first.

Players should recognize a profession before seeing details such as textures, colors, or facial features.

Silhouette readability always has higher priority than decorative details.

### Global Principles

Every class must have:

- Unique overall outline
- Unique weapon silhouette
- Unique center of gravity
- Unique equipment distribution
- Unique movement feeling

Avoid creating two professions with nearly identical outlines.

A player should recognize the profession even when viewed as:

- Black silhouette
- Small battlefield sprite
- Low resolution
- Fast combat

## Chapter 03 - Profession Evolution Language

Status: Review Draft

Purpose:

Define how each profession visually evolves through class progression.

The game does not use a paper-doll equipment system.

Visual progression is achieved through profession advancement rather than interchangeable equipment.

Every promotion should feel like a natural evolution of the previous class.

### Global Evolution Principles

Each promotion should gradually evolve:

- Armor complexity
- Clothing refinement
- Weapon identity
- Profession identity
- Civilization identity

Avoid sudden visual changes that make a promotion feel unrelated to its previous class.

Players should immediately recognize that a higher class evolved from its previous profession.
### Starting Profession Visual Rule

Purpose:

Define the visual identity of every starting profession (Promotion 1).

Promotion 1 represents the beginning of a character's journey and must leave significant room for future visual evolution.

#### Visual Age

Promotion 1 should present a visual age of approximately:

15-16 years old.

The character should appear youthful and inexperienced, but not childish.

Facial features should feel:

- Young
- Hopeful
- Determined
- Slightly inexperienced

Avoid mature or battle-hardened appearances.

#### Equipment Quality

Promotion 1 equipment should be:

- Standard issue
- Functional
- Mass-produced
- Kingdom military quality

Avoid:

- Royal craftsmanship
- Hero-grade equipment
- Legendary ornamentation
- Elite military decorations

#### Character Impression

Promotion 1 should communicate:

"A newly enlisted professional."

Not:

"A veteran soldier."

Not:

"A legendary hero."

#### Evolution Space

Promotion 1 must intentionally preserve visual growth potential.

Higher promotions should gradually improve through:

- Equipment refinement
- Material quality
- Silhouette expansion
- Civilization identity
- Signature elements

Promotion 1 should never consume these visual milestones.

#### Universal Rule

This rule applies to every starting profession, including:

- Guard
- Archer
- Rogue
- Mage
- Priest

Unless explicitly overridden by future world-building documents.

### Evolution Milestone Rule

Purpose:

Visual progression should not rely on adding more decorations at every promotion.

Instead, every profession shall evolve through clear visual milestones.

Players must immediately recognize major advancement even when viewing small battlefield sprites.

#### Three-Tier Evolution Structure

All professions follow three major visual eras.

##### Tier I

Promotion 1-3

Identity:

Beginning Adventurer

Visual goal:

- Simple
- Functional
- Light equipment
- Civilian-level craftsmanship
- Small visual improvements between promotions

##### Tier II

Promotion 4-6

Identity:

Professional Specialist

Visual goal:

- Strong profession identity
- Higher civilization quality
- Signature equipment
- Recognizable profession silhouette

Promotion 4 becomes the first major visual milestone.

Promotions 5-6 refine the new identity.

##### Tier III

Promotion 7-9

Identity:

Legendary Hero

Visual goal:

- Legendary craftsmanship
- Unique visual language
- Iconic equipment
- Maximum silhouette recognition

Promotion 7 becomes the second major visual milestone.

Promotions 8-9 refine the legendary identity.

#### Evolution Philosophy

Minor promotions should improve details.

Milestone promotions should redefine the profession.

Players should immediately recognize when a character enters a new visual era.

#### Battlefield Readability

The difference between Tier I, Tier II and Tier III must remain recognizable even at battlefield sprite size.

Visual progression should rely on silhouette, shape language and material language rather than excessive decorative details.

#### Universal Rule

Every profession must contain:

```text
Tier I
-> Major Visual Milestone
-> Tier II
-> Major Visual Milestone
-> Tier III
```

No profession should progress only by continuously adding ornaments.

Each milestone should introduce a clear visual identity shift.
### Visual Hierarchy Rule

Purpose:

Visual evolution should prioritize the most recognizable elements first.

Each profession shall have a clear hierarchy of visual importance.

New promotions should strengthen higher-priority elements before adding secondary decorative details.

#### Priority Levels

##### Priority 1 - Primary Silhouette

Examples:

- Shield
- Bow
- Staff
- Cape
- Mount
- Large weapon outline

This level creates the strongest battlefield recognition.

##### Priority 2 - Equipment Structure

Examples:

- Shoulder armor
- Chest armor
- Helmet
- Bracers
- Belt
- Boots

These reinforce the profession identity.

##### Priority 3 - Material Quality

Examples:

- Steel refinement
- Leather quality
- Cloth craftsmanship
- Wood carving
- Crystal integration

Material evolution supports progression but should not dominate recognition.

##### Priority 4 - Decorative Details

Examples:

- Engravings
- Embroidery
- Feathers
- Leaves
- Small ornaments
- Jewelry

Decorative elements should enhance the design, not define it.

No profession should rely primarily on decorative accumulation for visual progression.

#### Universal Rule

Every promotion should first improve:

```text
Primary Silhouette
-> Equipment Structure
-> Material Quality
-> Decorative Details
```

Visual identity must always take precedence over ornament density.

### Guard Line

Evolution direction:

```text
Kingdom Recruit
-> Professional Guard
-> Frontline Vanguard
-> Knight
-> Royal Knight
```

Visual evolution:

- Armor becomes heavier
- Shield becomes more refined
- Cape becomes longer
- Royal symbols gradually increase
- Overall silhouette becomes wider and more prestigious

### Archer Line

Evolution direction:

```text
Hunter
-> Experienced Archer
-> Elite Marksman
-> Master Archer
-> Royal Ranger
```

Visual evolution:

- Bow becomes longer and more elegant
- Quiver becomes more refined
- Nature elements increase gradually
- Armor remains lightweight
- Mobility remains the core identity

### Rogue Line

Evolution direction:

```text
Rogue
-> Wanderer
-> Branch Specialization
```

Visual evolution:

Early stage:

- Single Dagger
- Simple travel clothing
- Lightweight equipment

Night Branch:

```text
Single Dagger
-> Dual Daggers
-> Elite Assassin appearance
```

Intelligence Branch:

```text
Single Dagger
-> Hidden Blade
-> Fist Blades
-> Elite intelligence operative
```

Bard Branch:

```text
Single Dagger
-> Short Flute
-> Lute
-> Professional Bard appearance
```

Every branch should remain visually connected to Rogue while developing its own unique identity.

### Mage Line

Visual evolution:

- Robes become more refined
- Staff gains magical complexity
- Arcane ornaments gradually increase
- Magic focus becomes more recognizable

### Priest Line

Visual evolution:

- Holy robes become more ceremonial
- Holy symbols gradually increase
- Staff becomes more sacred
- Overall appearance becomes calmer and more dignified

### Universal Rule

Visual evolution should always feel continuous.

Promotion should communicate growth, not replacement.

Players should immediately recognize the profession lineage across every advancement.

## Chapter 04 - Shape Language

Status: Review Draft

Purpose:

定義各職業的幾何造型語言（Shape Language）。

Shape Language 並非角色輪廓（Silhouette），而是組成角色外型的設計語言。

即使玩家看不到材質、顏色與細節，也應能透過整體造型感受到職業特色。

Shape Language 的優先度高於裝飾細節。

### Guard Line（衛兵系）

核心印象:

- 穩定
- 可靠
- 紀律
- 王國軍隊

Shape Language:

主要幾何:

- 方形
- 矩形
- 粗直線

視覺特徵:

- 寬肩
- 厚重胸甲
- 大盾
- 直線披風
- 下盤穩定
- 重心偏低

避免:

- 過多尖角
- 過細身形
- 飄逸布料
- 不對稱設計

### Archer Line（弓箭手系）

核心印象:

- 靈巧
- 森林
- 流動
- 精準

Shape Language:

主要幾何:

- 細長矩形
- 弧線
- 流線

視覺特徵:

- 修長身形
- 長弓
- 長箭袋
- 輕披肩
- 布料流動感

避免:

- 過厚鎧甲
- 大面積盾牌
- 過於方正

### Rogue Line（遊蕩者系）

核心印象:

- 敏捷
- 神秘
- 旅行者
- 街頭生存

Shape Language:

主要幾何:

- 三角形
- 斜線
- 不對稱
- 前傾

視覺特徵:

- 前傾站姿
- 單側披肩
- 多腰包
- 交錯皮帶
- 輕量裝備

避免:

- 完全對稱
- 過重鎧甲
- 大盾牌
- 過多皇家裝飾

### Mage Line（法師系）

核心印象:

- 知識
- 奧術
- 神秘
- 理性

Shape Language:

主要幾何:

- 垂直
- 圓弧
- 下垂布料

視覺特徵:

- 長袍
- 長袖
- 法杖
- 法器
- 柔和線條

避免:

- 厚重鎧甲
- 大型盾牌
- 粗獷輪廓

### Priest Line（祭司系）

核心印象:

- 神聖
- 平衡
- 莊嚴
- 守護

Shape Language:

主要幾何:

- 圓形
- 對稱
- 垂直

視覺特徵:

- 聖袍
- 對稱披肩
- 聖杖
- 聖徽
- 柔和飄帶

避免:

- 過多尖角
- 暗殺風格
- 街頭元素
- 過度侵略感

### Universal Rule（通用規則）

Shape Language 應優先於:

- 顏色
- 材質
- 花紋
- 裝飾
- 配件

玩家應先透過整體造型辨識職業，再閱讀細節。

不同職業主系不得共用相同的 Shape Language。

Shape Language 描述的是職業主系（Guard、Archer、Rogue、Mage、Priest），不是每一個轉職。

同一主系的所有轉職，都應建立在相同的 Shape Language 上，再透過 Profession Evolution Language 逐步演化。

## Profession Master Reference Workflow

Status: Review Draft

Purpose:

Define the production workflow for all Profession Master References.

The workflow ensures every profession follows the same review process before artwork production.

### Step 1 - Complete the Profession Master Reference

All required sections must be filled.

No artwork is generated.

### Step 2 - Review and Approve the Written Visual Specification

Only after approval may concept artwork begin.

### Step 3 - Create Character Master Reference Artwork

Artwork must follow the approved Profession Master Reference.

No battlefield adaptation yet.

### Step 4 - Review the Character Master Reference Artwork

Adjust visual identity if necessary.

Do not begin sprite production.

### Step 5 - Create Battlefield Adaptation

Apply Battlefield Bible rules:

- 35° Floor-Direction View
- Rear-Dominant Readability
- Safe Area
- Mounted Rules
- Scale Rules

### Step 6 - Review Battlefield Adaptation

Only after approval may sprite production begin.

### Step 7 - Sprite Production

Sprite production is the final phase.

Sprites must not redefine the approved visual identity.

### Workflow Rule

```text
Written specification
-> Character Master Reference
-> Battlefield Adaptation
-> Sprite Production
```

No production phase may skip the previous review stage.

### Progressive Generation Rule

Purpose:

Character artwork should evolve progressively through each promotion.

Every promotion must be generated from the previously approved promotion artwork.

Do not regenerate every promotion independently.

#### Generation Workflow

```text
Promotion 1
-> Generate Promotion 2 using Promotion 1 as the primary visual reference
-> Generate Promotion 3 using Promotion 2 as the primary visual reference
-> Generate Promotion 4 using Promotion 3 as the primary visual reference
-> Continue until the final promotion
```

#### Design Philosophy

Each promotion represents the natural evolution of the same character.

The profession should gradually mature rather than suddenly change appearance.

Players should feel they are watching the same individual grow stronger over time.

#### Consistency Rule

Every new promotion should inherit from the previous promotion:

- Facial identity
- Hairstyle
- Armor language
- Material language
- Shape language
- Signature elements
- Overall silhouette

Only the intended evolutionary changes should be introduced.

#### Milestone Rule

Major visual milestones remain at:

- Promotion 4
- Promotion 7

Even during milestone promotions, visual continuity must remain clear.

Evolution should feel continuous rather than replacing the character.

#### Workflow Relationship

```text
Character Bible
-> Profession Master Reference
-> Promotion 1
-> Promotion 2
-> Promotion 3
-> ...
-> Promotion 9
```

Each promotion becomes the parent reference for the next promotion.

### Approval Gate Rule

Purpose:

Every promotion must be reviewed and approved before generating the next promotion.

No promotion may become the parent reference unless it has passed visual review.

#### Workflow

```text
Promotion 1
-> User Review
-> Approved
-> Promotion 2
-> User Review
-> Approved
-> Promotion 3
-> ...
-> Continue until the final promotion
```

#### Generation Rule

Only approved artwork may become the parent reference for the next promotion.

Rejected artwork must never be used as a reference.

#### Philosophy

Visual quality should be accumulated.

Visual mistakes should never propagate through the evolution chain.

Each promotion inherits from an approved visual foundation.

### Reference Lock Rule

Purpose:

Prevent outdated, rejected, or unrelated visual references from contaminating future artwork generation.

Only approved references may participate in progressive generation.

#### Approved Reference Sources

Artwork generation may only use:

1. Approved previous promotion artwork.
2. Approved Profession Master Reference.
3. Character Bible.
4. Battlefield Bible, only during Battlefield Adaptation.

#### Forbidden Reference Sources

Do not use:

- Rejected artwork
- Archived concepts
- Deprecated candidates
- Temporary experiments
- Old generated_images
- Historical PNGs
- Green-screen placeholder images
- Angle test images
- Comparison sheets
- Any artwork not explicitly approved

#### Parent Reference Rule

Every new promotion must inherit from exactly one approved parent promotion.

Do not mix multiple historical parents.

#### Clean Generation Rule

Every generation session should begin from the approved reference set only.

Do not automatically search historical folders for inspiration.

#### Review Rule

If a new parent reference is approved, it replaces the previous parent reference.

Rejected artwork never becomes part of the reference chain.

#### Philosophy

Visual consistency is achieved through controlled inheritance.

Quality is preserved by limiting reference sources.

### Status Rule

Do not promote to Active Rule.

## Profession Master Reference Template V1

Status: Review Draft

Purpose:

Standardize every Profession Master Reference.

All professions must follow the exact same structure.

### 01. Profession Identity

Defines:

- Core Fantasy
- Civilization Identity
- World Role
- Visual Personality

### 02. Scope

Lists all classes included in this profession family.

### 03. Character Bible References

Reference existing approved chapters only.

Do not duplicate rules.

### 04. Visual Identity Summary

Summarize the profession's visual philosophy.

### 05. Evolution Summary

Describe how the profession visually evolves across promotions.

### 06. Visual Keywords

5-10 keywords describing the profession.

Example:

- Heavy
- Royal
- Reliable
- Disciplined
- Protective


### 07. Signature Elements

Purpose:

Each profession shall define a set of permanent signature visual elements.

These elements represent the profession's most recognizable visual identity.

Signature Elements may evolve in quality and craftsmanship throughout promotions, but should not disappear.

Definition:

Signature Elements are physical visual features immediately recognized by players.

Examples include:

- Primary weapon type
- Signature shield
- Cape style
- Helmet style
- Cloak style
- Iconic accessories
- Profession-specific equipment

Design rule:

Signature Elements should remain consistent across all promotions.

Progression should refine, enhance, or legendary-upgrade these elements rather than replace them with unrelated designs.

Relationship:

Visual DNA defines the profession philosophy.

Signature Elements define the profession appearance.

Both must remain consistent throughout the entire profession family.
### 08. Things That Must Always Exist

List visual elements that should always remain recognizable.

### 09. Things That Must Never Exist

List visual elements that should never appear within this profession family.


### 10. Visual Boundaries

Purpose:

Define the acceptable range of visual evolution for a profession.

Visual evolution may expand the profession identity but must never replace its core identity.

Definition:

Visual Boundaries describe:

- What the profession is allowed to become
- What the profession should never become

This defines the long-term evolution limits across all promotions.

Design rule:

A profession may evolve through:

- Better craftsmanship
- More refined materials
- Higher civilization identity
- Larger silhouettes
- More recognizable signature elements

A profession should never evolve into another profession's visual identity.

Relationship:

Visual DNA defines the profession's core identity.

Signature Elements define its iconic appearance.

Visual Boundaries define how far evolution may go while remaining recognizable.

All three work together to maintain long-term visual consistency.
### 11. Reserved Sections

Reserved sections:

- Approved Master Reference Images
- Approved Color Palette
- Approved AI Generation References
- Battlefield Adaptation References
- Sprite Production References

### Production Rule

Templates define visual specifications only.

No concept generation.

No sprite generation.

No battlefield adaptation.

No production assets.

## Rogue Branch Weapon Progression

Status: Review Draft
Scope: Character design weapon language only

This section defines the currently approved Rogue weapon progression for visual identity review.

This section does not modify:

- Gameplay systems
- Godot scenes
- Runtime code
- Sprite pipeline
- Existing class progression
- Combat rules
- Skill rules

### Base Branch

| Class | Weapon |
|---|---|
| Rogue | Single Dagger |
| Wanderer | Single Dagger |

Design language:

Maintain a lightweight traveler identity during the early progression.

Avoid unnecessary weapon changes before specialization.

### Night Walker Branch

| Class | Weapon |
|---|---|
| Night Walker | Single Dagger |
| Shadow Walker | Dual Daggers |
| Assassin | Dual Daggers |
| Shadow Weaver | Dual Daggers |
| Devourer | Dual Daggers |
| Black Night | Dual Daggers |

Design language:

Dual Daggers become the first major visual evolution of the assassin line.

### Intelligence Branch

| Class | Weapon |
|---|---|
| Messenger | Single Dagger |
| Scout | Hidden Blade |
| Night Guard | Hidden Blade |
| Black Feather Operative | Fist Blades |
| Special Operations Captain | Fist Blades |
| Dark Falcon | Fist Blades |
| Dark Division Commander | Fist Blades |

Design language:

The intelligence branch evolves from concealed assassination tools into elite close-quarters combat weapons.

Weapon progression:

```text
Single Dagger
-> Hidden Blade
-> Fist Blades
```

### Bard Branch

| Class | Weapon |
|---|---|
| Messenger | Single Dagger |
| Singer | Short Flute |
| Performer | Short Flute |
| Bard | Lute |
| Melody Envoy | Lute |
| Ballad Keeper | Lute |
| Grand Balladeer | Lute |

Design language:

The musical branch gradually abandons conventional weapons.

Weapon progression:

```text
Single Dagger
-> Short Flute
-> Lute
```

The progression should feel natural rather than switching directly from dagger to a large instrument.

### Hidden Branch

Hidden Classes are excluded from the Closed Beta.

Do not produce concept art.

Do not establish final weapon language.

Reserve for a future major content update.


## Master Illustration Proportion

Status: Review Draft
Scope: Character Bible artwork proportion only

Purpose:

Define the standard body proportion for Character Bible illustrations, profession references, promotion artwork, codex portraits, promotional artwork, and future visual references.

Approved Review Candidate:

Master Illustration Body Ratio:

6.5-7 Head Proportion

Design intent:

- Elegant fantasy character proportions
- Semi-realistic anatomy
- Long but natural legs
- Readable armor and clothing details
- Suitable for profession reference artwork
- Suitable for future AI reference generation

This proportion is intended only for Character Bible artwork.

It is not the battlefield sprite proportion.

### Battlefield Separation

Battlefield characters follow Battlefield Bible rules.

Battlefield Sprite Ratio:

Approximately 3.5-4 Head Proportion

Purpose:

Prioritize gameplay readability over illustration detail.

The Character Bible and Battlefield Bible intentionally use different body proportions.
