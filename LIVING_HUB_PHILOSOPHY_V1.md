# LIVING_HUB_PHILOSOPHY_V1

Status:
OFFICIAL SOURCE OF TRUTH

This document defines the core philosophy of the Living Hub Tavern before any layout, artwork, Godot implementation, or gameplay interaction.

When this document conflicts with prompts or temporary Tavern design instructions, this document takes precedence unless explicitly superseded by a newer approved philosophy document.

---

## Purpose

Define the permanent design philosophy for the Living Hub Tavern.

This document establishes what the Tavern should feel like before any production decision is made.
It is not a layout document.
It is not an artwork specification.
It is not a Godot implementation plan.
It is not a gameplay interaction document.

---

## Orientation Rule

The Living Hub Tavern is a landscape gameplay scene.

Only the Battlefield uses portrait gameplay orientation.

Do not apply Battlefield portrait viewport assumptions to Tavern design.

Tavern camera movement, spatial composition, and future validation should be understood as horizontal movement through one continuous landscape Tavern interior.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

The Hub Tavern is not a game menu.

The Hub Tavern is a living place where the player's travelling guild returns after every expedition.

Players should feel they are returning home, not opening a function screen.

The Tavern should communicate continuity, warmth, daily life, and the presence of a travelling guild that exists beyond direct player input.

---

## 2. Living Hub

The Tavern exists continuously.

NPCs continue living whether or not the player interacts with them.

The Tavern should always appear:

- Active
- Comfortable
- Inhabited
- Warm
- Social
- Familiar

The Tavern should never feel like a frozen background with buttons placed on top.

Even when no player action is being taken, the space should still feel alive.

---

## 3. Player Freedom

The Tavern never forces a fixed interaction order.

Different players may naturally develop different daily routines.

Valid player behaviours include:

- Immediately starting another expedition.
- Checking newly arrived travellers.
- Collecting workshop production.
- Forging equipment.
- Organising the party.
- Simply watching the Tavern atmosphere.

All of these behaviours are considered correct.

The Tavern should support routine without forcing routine.
Players should feel free to decide what returning to the guild means each time.

---

## 4. Three-Zone Philosophy

The Tavern is divided into three emotional spaces.

These zones define emotional purpose before layout details.

### Left Zone

Purpose:
Discover today's world.

Contains:

- Travellers
- Bard activities
- Rumours
- Tavern records
- Outside atmosphere

The Left Zone should feel like the Tavern's connection to the wider world.

### Center Zone

Purpose:
Return to the travelling guild.

Contains:

- Round Table
- Inn
- Bartender
- Fireplace
- Core player party

This is the default login position.

The Center Zone should feel like home base.
It is the emotional heart of the Tavern.

### Right Zone

Purpose:
Witness the guild's growth.

Contains:

- Workshop
- Blacksmith
- Warehouse access
- Market entrance

The Right Zone should feel productive, practical, and connected to guild development.

---

## 5. NPC Philosophy

NPCs belong to the Tavern rather than to fixed function locations.

Blacksmiths, workers, travellers, bartenders, bards, and other residents may:

- Leave their workplaces.
- Rest.
- Drink.
- Chat.
- Walk around naturally.
- Participate in the daily atmosphere.

However, gameplay functionality must always remain immediately accessible regardless of NPC position.

Living behaviour must never create gameplay frustration.

NPC life should enrich the Tavern without hiding, delaying, or blocking core functions.

---

## 6. Camera Philosophy

The Tavern uses three camera focus zones.

Players gently move between:

- Left
- Center
- Right

Camera movement should feel like turning attention inside one Tavern rather than switching between different screens.

The camera should preserve the feeling of a continuous place.

Transitions should support presence, not interrupt it.

---

## 7. Atmosphere Philosophy

The Tavern should communicate life through subtle environmental behaviour.

Examples include:

- Fireplace burning.
- Bartender cleaning glasses.
- Travellers chatting.
- Bard performing.
- Steam from the workshop.
- Outside weather visible through windows and doors.

The environment should never feel static.

Atmosphere should support the feeling that the guild has returned to a living place.

Environmental life should be readable but not distracting.
It should create comfort, not noise.

---

## 8. Design Principle

Every future Tavern feature should answer one question:

"Does this make the Tavern feel more alive?"

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.

Feature usefulness alone is not enough.
The feature should also support the feeling of a living guild home.

---

## 9. Living Hub Statement

Players are not returning to a menu.

Players are returning to their travelling guild.

This statement is the core emotional test for every future Tavern decision.

---

## Future Review Items

- Define the final visual layout of the Left, Center, and Right Tavern zones.
- Define how NPC daily behaviour remains compatible with immediate function access.
- Define camera movement timing and focus behaviour for the three Tavern zones.
- Define atmosphere density so the Tavern feels alive without becoming visually noisy.
